Test the beta version

You're testing an early version of Manta Dive Log before it's officially released on the App Store. This helps me find bugs and improve the app – so occasional glitches or crashes may happen. That's completely normal, and exactly why I need beta testers like you. Thank you for taking part!

How to install the beta version for iOS

1

Install TestFlight

TestFlight is Apple's official app for installing beta versions – free on the App Store. If you don't have it yet:

Download here from App Store
2

Open the invite

Tap the button below. It opens TestFlight with the invitation to Manta Dive Log.

Join now

Best opened directly on the iPhone you want to use for testing – the link only works there, not on a computer.

3

Accept & install

In TestFlight, tap "Accept" and then "Install". Manta Dive Log then appears like a normal app on your home screen – recognizable by a small orange dot on the icon, TestFlight's marker for beta apps.

4

Get started

Open the app and try it out. Your feedback reaches me through the channels below.

Good to know

Test duration
Beta testing will end on ???. At that date all builds will be deactivated. If you plan on downloading and using the app after its official release in the App Store, remember to export and save your app data before then: Settings > Data > "create Manta-Backup". If possible, I will remind you before beta testing ends via a new build with an according message.
Build validity
A single beta build (beta app version) works for a maximum of 90 days. If the app stops launching after that, check for updates in the TestFlight app. If I upload a new build (e.g. having fixed one or two bugs), you'll be notified automatically via TestFlight. Dont worry: Your app data (dives, photos, etc.) survive when you update to a new build!
Giving feedback
Directly in TestFlight via "Send Beta Feedback". Also: Any screenshot taken in the Manta-Beta-app automatically gives the option "send Beta-Feedback", where you can mark, label, comment and send it directly via TestFlight. Or: by email to mantadivelog@gmail.com.
What should be tested?
  • General functionality of the app: Does it do what it's supposed to do
  • Design and navigation: Are the features and usability logical and intuitive?
  • Data persistence: Are values and data saved and displayed correctly — including across changes, exports, imports, etc.?
  • The translation: Anyone who's comfortable with English is welcome to use it in English and check that no leftover German text remains and that all translations are correct.
  • Does faulty or unexpected behavior occur anywhere?
  • Is anything missing, or what changes would you like to see?
